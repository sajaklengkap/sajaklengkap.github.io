5. 4. What semantic feature or property differentiates the following sets of nouns? a. Table, chair, pencil vs. water, dirt, cream (count vs. non.count) b. Mailman, nephew, priest vs. gander, stag, bull c. Niece, daughter, sister vs. nun, woman, girl d. Hen, ewe, cow vs. rooster, ram, bull e. Table, chair, pencil vs. love, thought, idea


Penjelasan:

The semantic feature or property that differentiates the following sets of nouns are:

a. Table, chair, pencil vs. water, dirt, cream: Countability. Table, chair, and pencil are count nouns, meaning they have a singular and plural form, while water, dirt, and cream are non-count nouns, meaning they are not typically counted and do not have a plural form.

b. Mailman, nephew, priest vs. gander, stag, bull: Animacy. Mailman, nephew, and priest are all human beings while gander, stag, and bull are all animals.

c. Niece, daughter, sister vs. nun, woman, girl: Kinship. Niece, daughter and sister all refer to familial relationships while nun, woman and girl do not.

d. Hen, ewe, cow vs. rooster, ram, bull: Gender. Hen, ewe and cow are all female animals while rooster, ram and bull are all male animals.

e. Table, chair, pencil vs. love, thought, idea: Abstractness. Table, chair and pencil are all concrete nouns that refer to physical objects while love thought and idea are abstract nouns that refer to intangible concepts.

Jawaban:

a. The semantic feature that differentiates the sets of nouns in a is countability. The first set (table, chair, pencil) consists of countable nouns, whereas the second set (water, dirt, cream) consists of non-countable nouns.

b. The semantic feature that differentiates the sets of nouns in b is gender. The first set (mailman, nephew, priest) consists of masculine nouns, whereas the second set (gander, stag, bull) consists of masculine animal nouns.

c. The semantic feature that differentiates the sets of nouns in c is relational status. The first set (niece, daughter, sister) consists of familial relationship nouns, whereas the second set (nun, woman, girl) consists of female gender nouns.

d. The semantic feature that differentiates the sets of nouns in d is gender and animal type. The first set (hen, ewe, cow) consists of female animal nouns, whereas the second set (rooster, ram, bull) consists of male animal nouns.

e. The semantic feature that differentiates the sets of nouns in e is abstractness. The first set (table, chair, pencil) consists of concrete nouns, whereas the second set (love, thought, idea) consists of abstract nouns.
